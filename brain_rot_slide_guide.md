# 🧠 Brain Rot Training — Slide-by-Slide Delivery Guide
### 24 Slides | 6 Stages | 24 Minutes

> Each slide entry contains:
> - **On Screen** — what participants see
> - **You Say** — speaker script
> - **How to Manage** — facilitation notes & timing

---

## STAGE 1 — What Is Brain Rot? | 0:00 – 4:00

---

### Slide 1 — Hook Opener
**⏱ 0:00 – 0:30**

**On Screen:**
> *"How many of you have opened a video app for 5 minutes… and looked up to find an hour had gone?"*

**You Say:**
"Quick question — be honest. Raise your hand if this has happened to you."

**How to Manage:**
- Pause after the question. Let hands go up.
- Smile and nod — laugh *with* them, not at them.
- Do NOT rush this. The silence is the hook.
- Transition: *"That feeling has a name now."*

---

### Slide 2 — The Definition
**⏱ 0:30 – 1:30**

**On Screen:**
```
Oxford Word of the Year — 2024

"Brain Rot"

The supposed deterioration of a person's mental or
intellectual state, especially as a result of overconsumption
of material considered to be trivial or unchallenging.
— Oxford University Press, 2024

📈 Usage increased 230% between 2023 and 2024
```

**You Say:**
"In 2024, Oxford Dictionary named 'brain rot' its Word of the Year. It beat out words like *demure* and *dynamic pricing*. And it's not a medical diagnosis — but it describes something very real that millions of people feel every day."

**How to Manage:**
- Read the definition slowly — let it sink in.
- Emphasize the 230% stat with a pause after it.
- Keep tone curious, not alarming.

---

### Slide 3 — Why Now?
**⏱ 1:30 – 2:30**

**On Screen:**
```
Why Is This Happening Now?

• Average screen time: 6 hrs 37 min per day
• Short-form video has tripled since 2020
• Gen Z: lower attention spans + higher anxiety
  than any generation before them
• Adults 30–50: fastest-growing group on TikTok & Reels

"That's why Oxford didn't call it 'teen rot'.
 They called it brain rot. Because it's all of us."
```

**You Say:**
"This isn't a new idea — but it's never been this widespread. The numbers tell us why 2024 was the tipping point."

**How to Manage:**
- The 30–50 stat usually surprises people. Pause after it.
- Look around the room as you say "it's all of us."
- This moment should feel inclusive, not accusatory.

---

### Slide 4 — Audience Question 1 + Recap
**⏱ 2:30 – 4:00**

**On Screen:**
```
💬 Question for You:

"When you first heard 'brain rot' —
 did it resonate? Did it feel familiar?"

🔁 Key Takeaways:
• Brain rot = mental decline from low-quality content overload
• It's cultural, documented, and growing
• You are not alone
```

**You Say:**
"I'd love to hear from 2 or 3 of you — when you first heard this term, did something click?"

**How to Manage:**
- Take 2–3 short responses max. Keep it moving.
- After responses, pivot to recap bullets on screen.
- Close with: *"So that's what it is. Next — why your brain can't seem to stop."*
- Hand off to Stage 2 presenter here if team-delivered.

## STAGE 2 — Your Brain on the Scroll | 4:00 – 8:00

---

### Slide 5 — The Dopamine Loop
**⏱ 4:00 – 5:30**

**On Screen:**
```
How Social Media Hijacks Your Brain

  Funny / shocking content
          ↓
    Dopamine spike 🔺
          ↓
    Dopamine drops — you feel empty
          ↓
    You scroll for the next hit
          ↓
       Loop repeats ♻️

"Same circuit as a gambling machine.
 Not a metaphor — literally the same."
```

**You Say:**
"Social media platforms are engineered by teams of engineers whose only job is to keep you watching. Here's how it works neurologically..."

**How to Manage:**
- Walk through the loop step by step — don't read it all at once.
- Land on the gambling machine line and pause for 2 seconds.
- Let the comparison do the work.

---

### Slide 6 — Audience Question 2
**⏱ 5:30 – 6:00**

**On Screen:**
```
💬 Think About This:

"Have you ever put your phone down feeling
 MORE tired or anxious than when you picked it up?

 What does that tell you?"
```

**You Say:**
"This is a yes or no for most people. Raise your hand if this sounds familiar."

**How to Manage:**
- Quick show of hands — 30 seconds max.
- Don't over-explain. Let them connect the dots.
- Transition: *"Here's what's actually changing in your brain."*

---

### Slide 7 — Brain Changes + Symptoms
**⏱ 6:00 – 7:30**

**On Screen:**
```
What Actually Changes in Your Brain

| What Happens           | The Effect                        |
|------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Constant novelty-seek  | Attention span: now avg 47 sec    |
| Dopamine desensitize   | Real tasks feel boring            |
| Shallow content diet   | Deep thinking becomes harder      |
| Notification anxiety   | Brain stuck in low-grade stress   |

Common Symptoms You May Recognize:
• Hard to focus for more than a few minutes
• Mental fatigue and procrastination
• Feeling restless without your phone
• Poor memory / reduced creativity
• Sleep problems from late-night scrolling
```

**You Say:**
"Your brain is neuroplastic — it rewires based on what you repeatedly do. And if you repeatedly scroll, it rewires for scrolling. Here's what that looks like in practice..."

**How to Manage:**
- Read the symptoms list slowly — leave space between each one.
- Watch the room — nodding and laughing means recognition. Good.
- Don't rush this slide. Recognition = engagement.

---

### Slide 8 — Audience Question 3 + Recap
**⏱ 7:30 – 8:00**

**On Screen:**
```
💬 Personal Reflection:

"What's one thing you used to enjoy
 that now feels harder to focus on?
 Reading? A full movie? A long conversation?"

🔁 Key Takeaways:
• Your brain didn't get lazy — it got hijacked
• This is by design, not your fault
• You're not weak. You're human.
```

**You Say:**
"Take 20 seconds — just think about that. You don't have to share."

**How to Manage:**
- Give genuine silence (20–30 sec). This is intentional.
- After the pause, move to the recap bullets calmly.
- Close: *"Now you know the mechanism. Next — what it's actually costing you."*

## STAGE 3 — The Real Cost | 8:00 – 12:00

---

### Slide 9 — At Work & In Learning
**⏱ 8:00 – 9:15**

**On Screen:**
```
Brain Rot Follows You Off Your Phone

At Work:
• 96 phone checks per day — once every 10 minutes
• 23 minutes to regain full focus after one interruption
• Constant context-switching = reacting, not thinking

For Students:
• Difficulty concentrating in lectures
• Avoidance of long reading or deep assignments
• Growing dependence on quick summaries
```

**You Say:**
"Brain rot doesn't stay in your social media apps. It follows you into your meetings, your deadlines, your exams."

**How to Manage:**
- The 23-minute stat consistently shocks people. Pause after it.
- Mention both workers and students — make it universal.


## STAGE 4 — 🎯 Activity: The Honest Mirror | 12:00 – 16:00

---

### Slide 13 — Instructions
**⏱ 12:00 – 12:30**

**On Screen:**
```
🎯 Activity: The Honest Mirror

A personal self-assessment — just for you.
No one sees your answers.

Rate yourself 1–5:
  1 = Never
  5 = Always

Be honest. The point isn't to feel bad.
It's to see clearly.
```

**You Say:**
"We're going to pause for a personal audit. No one will see your answers — this is just between you and yourself. Be honest."

**How to Manage:**
- Say "no one sees this" twice — it increases honest scoring.
- Hand out physical worksheets OR display the form on screen.
- In virtual sessions: share a Google Form link now.

---

### Slide 14 — Self-Assessment Form
**⏱ 12:30 – 14:00**

**On Screen:**
```
Rate each statement 1–5

1. I pick up my phone within 5 min of waking up       ___
2. I scroll when bored, waiting, or uncomfortable     ___
3. I've lost track of time while scrolling            ___
4. I feel restless or anxious without my phone        ___
5. I find it hard to focus without checking my phone  ___
6. I consume content I don't enjoy or find useful     ___
7. I feel drained/foggy after scrolling sessions      ___

                              TOTAL: _____ / 35
```

**You Say:**
"Take about 60 seconds. Read each one and score yourself honestly."

**How to Manage:**
- Go quiet. Let them fill it out — 60 full seconds of silence.
- Walk around the room (or monitor chat if virtual).
- Do not talk during filling time. Your silence signals this is real.

---

## STAGE 5 — The Exit Ramp | 16:00 – 20:00

---

### Slide 17 — The 3-Layer Strategy
**⏱ 16:00 – 17:30**

**On Screen:**
```
The Exit Ramp: 3 Layers of Change

Pick ONE layer to start — you don't need all three.

🔵 Layer 1 — Environment (Remove the Triggers)
   • Move social apps off your home screen
   • Turn off non-essential notifications
   • Charge your phone outside the bedroom

🟡 Layer 2 — Behavior (Break the Loop)
   • Ask: "Why am I reaching for this?" before every pickup
   • Protect one phone-free hour per day

🟢 Layer 3 — Replacement (Feed the Brain Better)
   • Swap one scroll session for a 5-min walk or stretch
   • Reintroduce a podcast, book, or documentary
```

**You Say:**
"There are three layers to escaping brain rot. You don't have to do all three at once — pick just one to start this week."

**How to Manage:**
- Go through each layer at a steady pace — don't rush.
- Emphasize "pick one" — overwhelm kills follow-through.

---

### Slide 18 — Question 5 + Is Tech the Enemy?
**⏱ 17:30 – 18:30**

**On Screen:**
```
💬 Question:

"Which layer would be hardest for you — and why?"

⚖️ Wait — Is Technology the Enemy?

No. The problem is not technology itself.

✅ Online learning, communication, research,
   creativity, educational content — all valuable.

❌ The issue: hours of passive, algorithm-driven
   scrolling with no intention.

"The goal isn't to quit technology.
 It's to use it — instead of being used by it."
```

**You Say:**
"Quick show of hands — which layer feels hardest? Layer 1, 2, or 3?"

**How to Manage:**
- Hands up for 20 seconds, then pivot to the "Is Tech the Enemy?" section.
- This nuance moment is important — deliver it warmly and clearly.
- It prevents people from dismissing the whole training as anti-tech.

```
Your Brain Can Recover

  That's withdrawal — and it passes within days
```

- The withdrawal line is reassuring — say "it passes within days" with confidence.

### Slide 21 — The Commitment Card
**⏱ 20:00 – 21:30**

**On Screen:**
```
✍️ My 1-Week Brain Detox Plan

Name (optional): ___________________
Date: ___________________

1. I will REMOVE this trigger:
   e.g., "Move Instagram off my home screen"
   → ________________________________

   e.g., "7–8am will be phone-free every day"
   → ________________________________

3. I will REPLACE one scroll session with:
   e.g., "A 10-min walk / 5 pages of a book"
   → ________________________________

In one week, I want to feel:
   → ________________________________
```

**You Say:**

- Walk around to signal this matters.
- In virtual: ask them to type in a private doc or on paper.

**⏱ 21:30 – 22:15**

**On Screen:**
```
🤝 Partner Share

Turn to the person next to you.

Share just ONE thing you wrote.
Not your score. Not your whole plan.
Just one commitment.

(Virtual: drop one line in the chat)
```
